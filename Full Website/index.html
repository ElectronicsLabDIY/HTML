PhD Research :
Surface Acoustic Waves (SAW) are elastic waves generated on free surface of solids. In engineering applications, SAW can be created via electromechanical transduction using piezoelectric materials. SAW devices are used in many applications such as: RF Filter, Touchscreen, MEMS sensor, Lab on chip device, fluid flow measurement and basic electronic building blocks. These applications are limited by its fixed frequency of operation. In this research, we are trying to make basic SAW device programmable by exploiting photoconductive property and advanced optical projection techniques. This will be highly useful for IOT and 5G applications along with the conventional need of SAW.

Surface acoustic wave (SAW) devices find their applications in electronics elementary functional units (eg- delay lines), radios (mixer, oscillator etc), microfluidics, flow measurement and for designing various electronics blocks in the communication system (amplifier, filters etc). If we take an example of a mobile phone in which we use bandpass filters just after the antenna and this bandpass filter is said to be best if it is having high Q-factor. Such filters are called high Q-BPF and this is achieved best using SAW devices. Some other areas and applications are also there where SAW devices are the best candidate when it comes to performance. The main problem with the SAW device is its fixed frequency characteristics. This limitation is due to fixed IDT structures which are made using metal such as Aluminium. When SAW waves are generated in SAW device, it is the pitch of the electrode which decides the frequency along with the acoustic wave velocity of material which is governed by two equations (i) f=v/ λ; where f, v and λ are the frequency, acoustic velocity and wavelength respectively, and (ii) λ=2p; where p is pitch of IDT (distance between two consecutive fingers of IDT). Similarly, filters are also designed for a particular center frequency due to the above reason. Thus, tuning of the filter is the major challenge with this device even though it got high Q property. Therefore. on one hand, we are getting high Q from SAW device and on the other hand, we are limited by tuning of frequency. Consequently, to materialize its high Q, one should make this device programmable. Not only this example but there are tons of application which can perform optimum if programmability can be added with the SAW device. If we talk about the solution then the solution to make it programmable can be found out in the above-discussed equations. For frequency tuning the direction to work it out maybe variable velocity or variable IDT pitch. To make a tunable SAW device one needs to use techniques such as - biasing the substrate to change velocity, exploiting photoconductive property for IDT implementation and external programming of IDTs. Photoconductive property in SAW device is not much explored yet with recently discovered or best available photoconductive materials and projection techniques. Using this technique with the advanced projection setup and by choosing suitable photoconductive material, a fully programmable and tunable SAW device can be achieved. The objective of this research is to explore various possible ways to make SAW device programmable.
